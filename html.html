About Cadbury Eclairs

Cadbury Eclairs are small, candy-coated chocolates that come in a variety of colors. The candy was first introduced in 1941 by Forrest Mars, Sr. and R. Bruce Murrie.

History

Cadbury Eclairs are a confectionery currently manufactured by Cadbury. Invented by the Birmingham-based confectionery company Taveners in 1932, they were adapted into a Dairy Milk version of Eclairs in 1965. They are available in bags or rolls and can be found in the Cadbury Heroes selection. Eclairs are currently available in the United Kingdom, Ireland, Australia, Kenya, Hong Kong, South Africa and India, where they are known as "Dairy Milk Eclairs".
